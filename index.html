Cat fact 1

The world's oldest known pet cat was found in a 9,500-year-old grave on the Mediterranean island of Cyprus, showcasing the long history of cats living alongside humans.

Cat fact 2

Cats have a unique grooming pattern, often licking their lips after a meal, which serves to clean their fur and regulate body temperature.

Cat fact 3

A group of cats is called a clowder, and within a clowder, each cat has a distinct vocalization that they use to communicate with each other.

Cat fact 4

Cats have a special reflective layer behind their retinas called the tapetum lucidum, allowing them to see well in low light conditions.

Cat fact 5

Cats have a strong territorial instinct and often mark their territory by rubbing scent glands located on their cheeks against objects, leaving their scent behind.

Cat fact 6

Contrary to popular belief, cats can be trained. They can learn commands, use a litter box, and even perform tricks when provided with positive reinforcement and patience.
